Team Abstract:
After COP27 was held in Egypt, climate change has become more apparent than ever, and it was a chance to look at the impacts of such a global challenge. It was found that water level, ph, TDS, and temperature were all impacted by climate change. In Egypt’s situation, where the Nile is split by one of the world’s longest rivers, it was decided by the team to study one of its impacts on fresh water. The goal of this research is to discover the relationship between global temperature changes and the TDS of water. To make sure that our needs to complete the study will be satisfied by our solution, design requirements were set. First, to make an interface that will graph a relation between the temperature and water salinity using a scatter plot graph and find the relationship between these two impacts. The other one is testing the range of the water temperature sensor. The collected data was analyzed by the team using the MATLAB user interface. As a result of our study, it was found that when the temperature is raised, the TDS is also raised. This correlation was represented on a scatter plot. At the end of this study, it was concluded that when water is boiled, the concentration of its TDS increases, and consequently, its salinity increases.
